About TESCOM

The Kogi State Teaching Service Commission (TESCOM) is a statutory body established by the government of Kogi State to oversee the recruitment, deployment, promotion, and welfare of teachers in public secondary schools across the state. Our mandate is to ensure that the educational sector particularly at the post-primary level is staffed with qualified, competent, and motivated teaching professionals who will deliver quality education to our students. TESCOM is committed to driving excellence in teaching by upholding standards, promoting professional development, and fostering a culture of accountability and innovation within the teaching workforce. We work in close collaboration with the Ministry of Education, Science and Technology and other stakeholders to implement educational policies, monitor school performance, and support teachers in achieving their full potential.

Our Services

Our Vision

To build a robust, professional, and inspired teaching workforce that drives academic excellence in all public secondary schools across Kogi State.

learn more

Our Mission

To recruit and retain highly qualified educators through a transparent and merit-based process. To promote continuous professional development for teachers. To enhance the welfare and career progression of teaching staff. To ensure the equitable distribution of teachers across all local government areas. To contribute to the overall development of the education sector in Kogi State.

learn more

What We Do

Conduct recruitment and placement of teachers in public secondary schools. Supervise and monitor teacher's performance and discipline. Facilitate promotions and career advancement for qualified teachers. Collaborate on teacher training programs and professional development workshops. Provide policy recommendations for improving teaching services in the state.
